About

Adrian Morawdi (Alireza Moradi) is an independent interdisciplinary literary researcher focusing on identity structures in literary narratives. His work connects conceptual modelling, philosophical frameworks, and narrative theory, with particular interest in the model he calls Quantum Character Identity.

His project Echoes of Adrian explores intersections of narrative identity, poetic expression, and cinematic thought.

Research

His current research aims to develop theoretical structures that explain multiplicity, identity fragmentation, and narrative coherence across literary forms. He integrates inspirations from philosophy and conceptual physics to propose new approaches in literary analysis.

  • Conceptual identity models in character construction
  • Narrative multiplicity and selfhood
  • Philosophical approaches to narrative theory
  • Poetic structures as identity carriers
